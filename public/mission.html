Mission and Purpose

The Telangana Human Rights Anti-Corruption and Crime Control Council (THRCCCC) is committed to protecting human rights, ensuring justice, fighting corruption, controlling crime, and building a safe, fair, and harmonious society.

  1. Work according to the Human Rights Protection Act, 1993 for the promotion and protection of human rights.
  2. Follow the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948) adopted by the United Nations.
  3. Protect fundamental rights given by the Indian Constitution, including equality, freedom, education, culture, and constitutional remedies.
  4. Build and operate educational institutions for human rights awareness.
  5. Organize camps, seminars, workshops, and conferences to educate citizens about their rights.
  6. Cooperate with NGOs and educational institutions working for human rights.
  7. Safeguard women's rights and ensure women's safety in colleges, workplaces, and public spaces.
  8. Prevent violence, harassment, and discrimination against women.
  9. Organize awareness programs to educate women about their rights.
  10. Work with National and State Commissions for Women to provide justice to victims.
  11. Protect child rights under the Child Rights Protection Act.
  12. Prevent child marriage, child violence, child labour, and child trafficking.
  13. Promote education for all children, especially orphans.
  14. Operate Child Improvement Centres and support shelters for vulnerable children.
  15. Empower youth through leadership, skill development, and education programs.
  16. Prevent and eradicate drug and narcotics abuse (including ganja, meth, and other illegal substances).
  17. Run awareness, prevention, and rehabilitation programs for youth involved in drugs.
  18. Encourage youth participation in nation-building and community service.
  19. Work with Central, State, and local governments for crime control and human rights protection.
  20. Assist police in maintaining peace, harmony, and goodwill in communities.
  21. Provide justice to victims through NHRC, SHRC, NCW, SCPCR, courts, and other bodies.
  22. File petitions in court for human rights violation cases.
  23. Monitor and prevent cybercrime, human trafficking, bonded labour, and other crimes.
  24. Promote corruption-free governance in partnership with government agencies.
  25. Use the Right to Information (RTI) Act to expose corruption.
  26. Take legal action against those involved in corrupt practices.
  27. Educate citizens about their rights to demand transparency.
  28. Set up Old Age Homes, Health Centres, Nari Shakti Kendras, and Hospitals.
  29. Organize medical camps, blood donation camps, and eye check-up camps.
  30. Conduct awareness drives against dowry, caste discrimination, and communal hatred.
  31. Honour individuals and organizations contributing to society.
  32. Assist citizens in submitting complaints to authorities and ensure proper resolution.
  33. Work with government departments, commissions, and NGOs to implement social and legal reforms.
  34. Support awareness and publicity for government welfare schemes.
  35. Assist in public grievance redressal, crime investigation, and security services.
  36. Help the administration prevent misuse of government positions and resources.

About Us

THRCCCC is a non-government, non-profit organisation registered under the Indian Trusts Act, 1882. We are connected with NITI Aayog. We work every day to protect people's rights, stop corruption, and control crime so that Telangana can be a safe, fair, and peaceful place for everyone.
