Right to Education

Right to Education in India

The Right to Education (RTE) is a fundamental right guaranteed by the Constitution of India under Article 21A, which was added through the 86th Constitutional Amendment Act, 2002. This right ensures that every child between the ages of 6 and 14 years has access to free and compulsory education.

To implement this constitutional provision, the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009 – commonly known as the RTE Act, 2009 – came into force on 1 April 2010.

Key Provisions of the RTE Act, 2009

  1. Free and Compulsory Education – No child between 6 and 14 years shall have to pay any fees or charges to receive elementary education.
  2. Neighbourhood Schools – Every child must be provided a school within a reasonable distance from their home.
  3. Quality Standards – Schools must meet specific infrastructure and teacher-student ratio standards.
  4. Reservation for Disadvantaged Groups – 25% reservation in private unaided schools for children from economically weaker sections and disadvantaged groups.
  5. No Discrimination or Harassment – Children cannot be discriminated against based on caste, gender, religion, disability, or economic background.
  6. No Detention and No Expulsion – No child can be held back or expelled until the completion of elementary education.
  7. Special Training – Out-of-school children must be provided special training to bring them at par with their age-appropriate class.

Constitutional Importance

The inclusion of Article 21A reflects India’s commitment to the principle that education is the foundation of democracy, equality, and empowerment. The RTE Act not only ensures access to education but also focuses on equity, quality, and inclusiveness, aiming to bridge social and economic gaps.

About Us
