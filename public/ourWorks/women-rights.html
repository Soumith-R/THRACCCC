Women rights

Women's Rights, Safety, and Legal Protections

The Telangana Human Rights Anti-Corruption and Crime Control Council (THRCCCC) works in line with the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948), adopted by the United Nations, which affirms that all human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights. We also uphold the Indian Constitution, which guarantees several rights to all citizens, regardless of gender – such as the Right to Equality (Article 14) and the Right to Life and Personal Liberty (Article 21).

Gender Equality and Women's Empowerment

Gender equality means that both women and men have equal opportunities, rights, and responsibilities in all areas of life – decision-making, economic and social freedom, access to education, and the freedom to choose one's profession.

To achieve true gender equality, it is vital to empower women economically, socially, and politically, ensuring their safety and dignity in public spaces, workplaces, and educational institutions.

Key Constitutional Rights for Women in India

  1. Equality before law – Article 14 ensures equal protection of laws.
  2. Prohibition of discrimination – Article 15 prohibits discrimination on the basis of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth, and Article 15(3) allows special provisions for women and children.
  3. Equality in employment – Article 16 guarantees equal opportunity in public employment without discrimination on gender.
  4. Freedom of speech and profession – Article 19 ensures the right to express opinions and choose any lawful profession.
  5. Protection of life and personal liberty – Article 21 safeguards the right to live with dignity and privacy.
  6. Right to property – Article 300-A provides the right to own and hold property.
  7. Political rights – Reservation for women in local governance such as Panchayats.
  8. Free legal aid – Under the Legal Services Authorities Act, women and children are entitled to free legal assistance.
  9. Directive Principles of State Policy – Provide for equal pay, adequate livelihood, free and compulsory education, and public health improvement.

Important Legal Rights Every Woman Should Know

  1. Right to free legal aid – Women are entitled to a lawyer, especially in sensitive cases such as sexual assault, and cannot be denied legal representation.
  2. Right to privacy while recording statements – Under Section 164 of CrPC, statements in rape cases can be recorded in private before a magistrate or in the presence of a woman officer.
  3. Right to file an FIR at any time – Delay in reporting does not invalidate the complaint. The police must register an FIR regardless of the time elapsed.
  4. Right to complain via email or registered post – If unable to visit a police station, a written complaint can be sent to senior police officials, who must ensure action is taken.
  5. Zero FIR – Women can file a complaint at any police station, regardless of jurisdiction.
  6. No arrests after sunset – Women cannot be arrested at night except under exceptional circumstances with a magistrate's approval.
  7. No police station summons – Under Section 160 of CrPC, women cannot be called to a police station for interrogation; questioning must be done at their residence in the presence of family or a woman constable.
  8. Protection of identity – Section 228-A of IPC prohibits disclosure of a rape victim's identity.
  9. Medical rights – Under Section 164A of CrPC, rape victims must be medically examined, and the medical report must be shared with them.
  10. Workplace protection – All organizations must have a Sexual Harassment Complaints Committee headed by a woman with at least 50% women members, including one from a women's welfare group.

THRCCCC's Role in Protecting Women's Rights and Safety

  • Awareness in Colleges and Schools – Conduct workshops on women's safety, legal rights, and self-defense.
  • Safety from Harassment – Work with law enforcement to ensure a harassment-free environment in public and private institutions.
  • Support for Victims – Provide assistance in filing complaints, securing legal aid, and ensuring privacy in sensitive cases.
  • Monitoring Law Implementation – Work with government bodies to ensure compliance with laws protecting women's rights.
  • Public Awareness Campaigns – Use media, seminars, and community outreach to inform women of their rights.
  • Collaboration with Authorities – Partner with police, legal services, and NGOs to protect women from violence and discrimination.
