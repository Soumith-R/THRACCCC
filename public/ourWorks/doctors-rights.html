Doctors rights

Rights of Doctors in India

Doctors in India play a vital role in protecting public health and saving lives. To ensure they can perform their duties without fear or interference, they are granted certain legal and constitutional rights. These rights are derived from fundamental freedoms under the Indian Constitution, as well as from various laws, medical ethics codes, and professional regulations.

Key Rights of Doctors

  1. Right to Practice Profession (Article 19(1)(g) of the Constitution)
    Every doctor has the constitutional right to practice their profession anywhere in India, subject to compliance with the Indian Medical Council Act, 1956 (now replaced by the National Medical Commission Act, 2019) and the rules of the National Medical Commission (NMC).
  2. Right to Life and Safety (Article 21 of the Constitution)
    Doctors have the right to work in a safe environment free from violence, threats, or harassment. Many states have enacted Medical Protection Acts to protect doctors and healthcare institutions from attacks by patients or their relatives.
  3. Right to Professional Autonomy
    Doctors have the right to make independent medical decisions based on their knowledge, ethics, and the patient’s best interests, without undue interference.
  4. Right to Fees for Services
    Doctors have the right to charge reasonable fees for their professional services, as agreed upon with the patient, while following ethical guidelines.
  5. Right to Refuse Treatment (in Certain Situations)
    Except in emergencies, a doctor has the right to refuse treatment if:
    • The patient does not follow medical advice.
    • The doctor’s safety is at risk.
    • The treatment goes against medical ethics.
  6. Right to Protection of Reputation (Article 21)
    Doctors have the right to protect their professional reputation from false accusations, defamation, or malicious complaints.
  7. Right to Confidentiality
    As per medical ethics and Section 23 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872, doctors have the right (and duty) to maintain the confidentiality of patient information, unless disclosure is required by law.
  8. Right to Legal Protection
    Doctors are protected under various provisions of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) and state laws from frivolous criminal prosecution for acts done in good faith during the course of treatment.
  9. Right to Work Without Discrimination
    Under Article 14 and Article 15 of the Constitution, doctors have the right to equal treatment and non-discrimination in employment, promotions, and professional opportunities.

Constitutional and Legal Support

The rights of doctors are backed by:

  • Article 19(1)(g) – Freedom to practice any profession.
  • Article 21 – Right to life and personal liberty, ensuring safety and dignity.
  • National Medical Commission Act, 2019 – Regulates medical education, ethics, and registration.
  • Medical Protection Acts in various states – Provide legal safeguards against violence in healthcare.
  • Indian Penal Code – Provides remedies for assault, criminal intimidation, and defamation.
