Right to Information Act 2005

About the Right to Information (RTI)

The Right to Information Act, 2005 guarantees citizens the legal right to request and receive information from government bodies in a timely manner. It is an important step towards transparency, accountability, and good governance in India.

The Act was introduced by the Department of Personnel and Training, Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances and Pensions, and established the RTI Portal Gateway. This platform enables citizens to:

  • Search details of Public Information Officers (PIOs) and First Appellate Authorities.
  • Access RTI-related information and disclosures published by various public authorities at both central and state levels.

Our Work in the Field of RTI

The Telangana Human Rights Anti-Corruption and Crime Control Council (THRCCCC) is committed to:

  1. Promoting awareness of the RTI Act among the public.
  2. Bringing RTI activists together to work in a united and coordinated manner.
  3. Acting as a central body for supporting RTI activists and initiatives.
  4. Training and guiding RTI activists to use the law effectively.
  5. Ensuring the security and protection of RTI activists.
  6. Monitoring the implementation and usage of the RTI Act.
  7. Advocating for improvements in RTI legal provisions to make them more effective.
  8. Making RTI a mass movement by encouraging citizens to demand transparency.
  9. Organizing seminars, workshops, public meetings, and campaigns related to RTI.
  10. Publishing awareness materials on RTI rights and usage.
  11. Recognizing and awarding outstanding RTI activists for their contributions.

Summary of the RTI Act, 2005

The RTI Act provides a practical framework for citizens to access information under the control of public authorities. It aims to:

  • Promote transparency and accountability in the functioning of all public authorities.
  • Establish the Central Information Commission and State Information Commissions to handle RTI matters.
  • Ensure an informed citizenry to strengthen democracy.
  • Act as a tool to fight corruption and hold governments accountable.
  • Balance transparency with the need to protect sensitive information and ensure efficient governance.

In short, the RTI Act empowers citizens to seek information, question decisions, and demand accountability, making it one of the strongest tools for a transparent and corruption-free democracy.
