Consumer Rights

Consumer Rights

  1. Right to Safety
    Consumers have the right to be protected from goods and services that may be harmful to life or property. Products and services should not only meet immediate needs but also be safe for long-term use. Before buying, consumers should check for quality and guarantees, and whenever possible, choose certified products like those with ISI or AGMARK marks.
  2. Right to be Informed
    Consumers have the right to receive complete and accurate information about a product or service, including its quality, quantity, purity, price, and standards. This helps prevent unfair trade practices and allows consumers to make wise, responsible decisions, avoiding high-pressure sales tactics.
  3. Right to Choose
    Consumers have the right to select from a range of goods and services at competitive prices. Even in cases of monopoly, they are entitled to satisfactory quality and fair pricing. This right ensures access to essential goods and services and is best exercised in a competitive market where various options are available.
  4. Right to be Heard
    Consumers' interests must be considered in decision-making forums. They should have representation in committees or organizations focused on consumer welfare. Forming independent, non-political, and non-commercial consumer groups can strengthen this right.
  5. Right to Seek Redressal
    Consumers have the right to fair compensation and settlement when subjected to unfair trade practices or exploitation. Even if a complaint seems minor, it may have a significant impact on society as a whole. Consumer organizations can provide support in pursuing grievances.
  6. Right to Consumer Education
    Consumers have the right to learn about their rights and responsibilities to make informed choices. Awareness is especially important in rural areas where exploitation is more common. Educated consumers are empowered to protect themselves and contribute to fair trade practices.

About Us

THRCCCC is a non-government, non-profit organisation registered under the Indian Trusts Act, 1882. We are connected with NITI Aayog. We work every day to protect people's rights, stop corruption, and control crime so that Telangana can be a safe, fair, and peaceful place for everyone.
