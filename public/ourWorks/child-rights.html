Child rights

Child Rights and Protection

Many children in our society face abandonment, lack of education, homelessness, and constant exposure to violence. They often live without access to primary healthcare, safe shelter, or even basic nutrition. Every day, countless children suffer neglect, abuse, and cruel treatment, despite being innocent, young, and full of potential. These children are being denied their fundamental rights.

The United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC) is the most widely ratified human rights treaty in history. It defines child rights as the minimum entitlements and freedoms owed to every person below the age of 18, regardless of race, nationality, or background. These rights ensure that children can live with dignity, be protected from harm, and have opportunities to develop their full potential.

Human Rights for All, Including Children

Human rights are built on the principles of equality and fairness. They grant individuals the freedom to make choices, live without fear or discrimination, and enjoy essential protections. Globally recognized human rights include:

  • The right to life
  • The right to a fair trial
  • Freedom from torture and cruel treatment
  • Freedom of speech and religion
  • The right to health, education, and an adequate standard of living

These rights are universal – they belong to everyone, everywhere, regardless of gender, age, wealth, beliefs, or origin. For children, they also extend to protection, development, participation, and survival.

Four Core Classifications of Child Rights under UNCRC

1. Right to Survival

  • The right to be born
  • Minimum standards of food, shelter, and clothing
  • The right to live with dignity
  • Access to healthcare, safe drinking water, nutritious food, and a clean environment
  • Information to help them stay healthy

2. Right to Protection

  • Protection from all forms of violence, abuse, and neglect
  • Protection from physical and sexual exploitation
  • Protection from child labour and trafficking

3. Right to Participation

  • Freedom of opinion and expression
  • Freedom of association
  • Access to information
  • Participation in decisions affecting their lives

4. Right to Development

  • The right to education and learning
  • Opportunities for emotional, mental, and physical development
  • The right to rest, play, and engage in cultural and recreational activities

Impact of the UNCRC

The Convention on the Rights of the Child is a milestone in global human rights history, placing children’s needs and rights at the centre of both international and national agendas. It has inspired laws, policies, and programs worldwide aimed at improving child survival, education, and protection.

Over decades, the UNCRC has led to:

  • Greater access to education and healthcare
  • Stronger laws safeguarding children’s best interests
  • Improved survival rates for children in many countries

However, challenges remain. In developing countries, including India, many children still face poverty, exploitation, and neglect. While laws and policies exist, implementation and enforcement are often lacking. Social barriers, inadequate resources, and weak monitoring systems slow progress.

THRCCCC's Call to Action

In Telangana and across India, there is an urgent need to:

  • Strengthen enforcement of child protection laws
  • Improve access to education, healthcare, and safe environments
  • Eliminate child labour, trafficking, and exploitation
  • Support orphaned and vulnerable children through shelters and rehabilitation
  • Promote community awareness and engagement in safeguarding children’s rights

By fully implementing the principles of the UNCRC, we can work towards creating a safe, nurturing, and empowering environment where every child can grow, learn, and thrive.
